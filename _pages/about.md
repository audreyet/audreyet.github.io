Audrey has been involved in sanctions research since 2021. Most recently, she co-authored two forthcoming reports on sanctioned actors involved in Southeast Asia’s cyber-scam, human trafficking, and money laundering industries for the Global Initiative Against Transnational Organized Crime. Her research on sanctions evasion and international timber markets was published in Foreign Policy magazine. Previously, she authored an annotated bibliography on the humanitarian and human rights impact of sanctions, commissioned by the Advancing Humanitarianism through Sanctions Refinement project and Fourth Freedom Forum.

Audrey has conducted extensive research on illicit markets and corruption in Southeast Asia, with six years’ fieldwork in Cambodia and Myanmar. She holds a Master of Global Affairs degree with a concentration in international peace studies from the University of Notre Dame and is commencing her Ph.D. in political science and international relations at Brown University this fall.
