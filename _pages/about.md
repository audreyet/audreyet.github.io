Audrey is a researcher of illicit markets, corruption, and conflict. Audrey has conducted extensive research on illicit markets and corruption in Southeast Asia, with six years’ fieldwork in Cambodia and Myanmar, and is fluent in Khmer. As a consultant for the Global Initiative Against Transnational Organized Crime, she co-authored two forthcoming reports on sanctioned actors and criminal networks in Southeast Asia involved in cyber-scams, human trafficking, and money laundering. Her research on sanctions evasion and international timber markets was published in Foreign Policy magazine. Previously, she authored an annotated bibliography on the humanitarian and human rights impact of sanctions, commissioned by the Advancing Humanitarianism through Sanctions Refinement project and Fourth Freedom Forum.

She holds a Master of Global Affairs degree with a concentration in international peace studies from the University of Notre Dame and B.A. from Goshen College. In fall 2025, she is commencing her Ph.D. in political science and international relations at Brown University.
